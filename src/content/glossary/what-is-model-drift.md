## What is Model Drift?

Model drift is a phenomenon where artificial intelligence (AI) models become less accurate over time. This happens because the data used to train the model changes, and the model doesn't adapt to these changes. Think of it like a map that was created last year - it might not show the new roads or buildings that were added recently. In the same way, an AI model trained on last year's data might not perform well on this year's data. For instance, a model that predicts customer behavior might be trained on data from a time when people's shopping habits were different. If people's habits change, the model's predictions will become less accurate. This is why model drift is a significant issue in many industries, including finance, healthcare, and marketing.

## Think of It Like This

Imagine you're a store owner, and you hired a consultant to help you understand your customers' shopping habits. The consultant created a report based on data from last year's sales. However, this year, a new store opened across the street, and your customers' shopping habits changed. The consultant's report is no longer accurate, and you need a new report based on this year's data. Similarly, AI models need to be updated with new data to remain accurate. Another way to think about it is to consider a weather forecast model. If the model is trained on data from a specific region and time period, it might not perform well when applied to a different region or time period. The model needs to be updated with new data to adapt to the changing weather patterns.

## Why Should You Care?

Model drift affects many aspects of our daily lives, from the products we see advertised online to the medical diagnoses we receive. For instance, if a medical diagnosis model is not updated with new data, it might miss new symptoms or fail to account for changes in disease patterns. This can have serious consequences for patients. In the financial sector, model drift can lead to inaccurate predictions of stock prices or credit risk, resulting in significant losses. Furthermore, model drift can also affect the recommendations we receive from streaming services or online retailers. If the models used to generate these recommendations are not updated, they might suggest products or shows that are no longer relevant to our interests.

## Where You've Already Seen It

Model drift is a common issue in many popular tools and services. For example, have you ever noticed that the ads you see on social media seem less relevant over time? This might be due to model drift, where the AI model used to target ads is no longer accurate. Another example is music streaming services like Spotify, which use AI models to recommend songs based on our listening habits. If these models are not updated, they might suggest songs that are no longer to our taste. Additionally, virtual assistants like Siri or Alexa might struggle to understand our voice commands if their models are not updated with new data.

## The One Thing to Remember

The key to understanding model drift is to recognize that AI models are not static entities - they need to be updated and adapted to changing data and circumstances. This is crucial to ensuring that AI models remain accurate and effective in their decision-making. By acknowledging the potential for model drift, we can take steps to mitigate its effects and ensure that AI models continue to provide valuable insights and services.
