## What is 3D Gaussian Splatting?

3D Gaussian Splatting is a technique used to create 3D models from a set of data points. It's like building with LEGO blocks, but instead of blocks, you're using tiny 3D blobs called splats. These splats are distributed in space to represent the shape of an object. The technique works by placing a small 3D Gaussian distribution, or a "splat," at each data point. This distribution gives the splat a size, shape, and orientation, which helps to capture the details of the object being modeled. The result is a smooth, detailed 3D model that can be used in various applications, such as computer-aided design, video games, or special effects.

Imagine you're trying to recreate a sculpture from a set of photographs. You could try to glue together small balls of clay to form the shape, but it would be hard to get it just right. 3D Gaussian Splatting is like using a special kind of clay that can be molded and shaped to fit the contours of the sculpture. This technique is useful for creating models of complex objects, like trees or animals, where traditional modeling methods might be difficult or time-consuming.
